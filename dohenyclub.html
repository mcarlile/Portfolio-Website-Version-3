The Doheny Club

Fall 2013

The Project

Edward Lawrence Doheny struck oil at the La Brea Tar Pits in Los Angeles in 1892. The same wealth that built USC’s Doheny Library caused his family to disintegrate, his son’s mysterious murder and the Teapot Dome Scandal. The Doheny Club is a long-term, persistent alternate reality game that weaves a trail across Los Angeles and throughout history in search of the legacy of Edward Lawrence Doheny.

Design Goal

The goal of this project was to design the interface for an alternate reality game that would artificially create a campus-wide, cult-like, pseudo-religious, non- logical obsession with Edward Doheny based on, but not limited by, historical facts.

Platform

A smartphone is required, as the game needs access to an accelerometer, camera, wifi, bluetooth and location services.

Player Objectives

Accessing the Game

Hints are placed around campus with links to download the application. This could include a phone number users text a message to written on a bathroom wall, QR codes taped on the doors of the library, etc. Anywhere completely in sight, but not conspicuous or out of place is fair game.

Solving Puzzles

Once players join the game, they are given cryptic puzzles to decode within the application. Puzzles point to specific locations that were important in Doheny’s life.

Finding Geocaches

To progress through the game, players must travel to the specific locations encoded in the puzzles and accomplish certain tasks. This could involve visiting the Doheny mansion and connecting via Bluetooth to a hidden raspberry pi mini computer, calling a phone number from a phone booth at the La Brea Tar Pits, or finding a specific book in the book stacks in Doheny.

Joining the Doheny Club

Players who complete all challenges are invited to a secret Google Group and are initiated into the Doheny Club. What happens next is up to them.
