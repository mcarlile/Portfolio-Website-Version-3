Nike Digital Campus Competition

Spring 2013

The Project

In early 2013, Nike sent out a request for proposal to student groups around the country. The company tasked these teams with developing a campaign to promote the 2014 World Cup in Brazil.

My partner, Eric Watkins, and I decided to create a campaign that centered around a global alternate reality game using Nike Fuel. We wanted to construct a diruptive, comprehsnive digital game that let Nike create persistent, personal relationships with its consumers. He focused on strategy while I designed the game and mocked up a mobile app interface.

Out of 45 national teams, our team placed third.

A Global Game

Nike+ uses accelerometers in mobile devices and wearable computers to convert physical activity to Fuel. When users workout using Nike+, they support their country in the world’s largest, most persistent sporting event.

Frictionless Sign Up

The Nikefuel World Cup lets users sign in with a Nike+ or FaceBook account. This sign up process lets Nike tailor the game experience to a user’s specific location, age, and gender.

"The digital and physical worlds are starting to come together more seamlessly. It’s only the tip of the iceberg in terms of what’s coming."

- Nike CEO Mark Parker

Personalized Shopping

The NikeFuel World Cup helps Nike sell 2014 World Cup Nike shoes, clothing and gear by building a personalized shopping experience inside an application that customers use every day.

Calling Users to Action

Massive LED leaderboards outside each of the 12 World Cup stadiums in Brazil encourage sustained, consistent engagement with the application and the NikeFuel World Cup Universe.
